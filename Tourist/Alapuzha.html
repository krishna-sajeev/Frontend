Welcome to Alappuzha

Alappuzha, also known by its anglicized name Alleppey, is one of the most captivating and picturesque districts in the Indian state of Kerala. Known as the “Venice of the East”, Alappuzha is a mosaic of shimmering backwaters, tranquil lakes, long stretches of paddy fields, and swaying coconut palms. The charm of Alappuzha lies not just in its scenic landscape but also in its rich cultural heritage, timeless traditions, and the warm hospitality of its people.
A Glimpse into History
Alappuzha has a fascinating history rooted in maritime trade. During the 18th and 19th centuries, it served as a bustling port town under the rule of Raja Keshawadasan, the Diwan of Travancore. Its strategically important location made it a major center for spice trade and coir products. The town was among the first in Kerala to adopt planned urban development and saw the early establishment of canals, warehouses, and lighthouses, some of which still stand today as silent witnesses to its vibrant past.

Cultural Heritage and Local Life

  • Coir Industry and Handicrafts
    • Alappuzha is the center of Kerala’s coir industry, with women-led cooperatives producing a variety of products from coconut fiber — mats, ropes, carpets, and handicrafts. A visit to a coir-making unit offers insight into this age-old skill and its importance to the local economy.
  • Cuisine
    • The local cuisine is a treat for the senses. Traditional meals are served on banana leaves and often include rice, fish curry, avial, and tangy pickles. Seafood lovers can enjoy fresh catches like prawns, mussels, and crab, prepared with coconut milk and aromatic spices. Don’t miss the tender coconut water and toddy (a local palm wine), which are refreshing and culturally iconic.
  • Best Time to Visit Alappuzha
    • The ideal time to visit is during the cooler months from October to March. This period offers pleasant weather for houseboat stays, sightseeing, and beach visits. The monsoon season (June to September), while humid, transforms the region into a lush green paradise and is preferred by Ayurvedic wellness seekers.
  • Accommodation Options
    • Alappuzha caters to all kinds of travelers. From luxury resorts and heritage homes to budget homestays and houseboats, there is something for everyone. Many properties are built in traditional Kerala architecture, adding charm to your stay.
  • Connectivity
    • By Air: The nearest airport is Cochin International Airport, about 83 km away. By Train: Alappuzha railway station is well connected to major cities. By Road: National Highway 66 passes through the district, connecting it to Kochi, Kollam, and Thiruvananthapuram. By Water: Alappuzha is also accessible via Kerala’s unique inland water transport system.

Why Visit Alappuzha?

Alappuzha offers a refreshing break from the chaos of urban life. Whether you're drifting in a houseboat under starlit skies, watching boat races with your heart racing, walking along breezy beaches, or discovering the rhythms of rural life, Alappuzha has something magical for every traveler. It’s not just a destination; it’s an experience woven with nature, culture, and unforgettable moments.
